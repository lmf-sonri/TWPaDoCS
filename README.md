### Towards Women Professors at DoCS

*Abstract* When the author first visited Uppala University in the
early 2000’s, she was slightly surprised and mildly disappointed that
there were no women professors at the Division of Computer
Systems. Over the following twenty years, DoCS has failed to hire and
retain even one woman who would go on to be promoted to professor at
UU. The situation should no longer be considered “mildly
disappointing”. It should be seen as evidence of a problem, especially
in context of a recent employee survey reporting no gender
discrimination at DoCS.

This essay discusses some gender-related aspects of hiring and
retaining women researchers, as well as issues related to evaluating
and improving the gender environment. While the focus is on DoCS, some
discussion is more broadly applicable in the IT department and
beyond. It is written from the perspective of an experienced Computer
Systems researcher, rather than a specialist in gender studies or HR
policy and so closes with some personal reflections.
